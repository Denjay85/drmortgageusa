VA Loan Credit Score Requirements in Florida: What You Actually Need in 2026

By Dennis Ross, NMLS #2018381 | March 3, 2026

One of the most common questions I get from Florida veterans: "What credit score do I need for a VA loan?" The short answer is that the VA itself does not set a minimum credit score. The longer answer is that your lender absolutely does, and those minimums vary more than most people realize.

Understanding the difference between VA guidelines and lender overlays is the key to getting approved, especially if your score is below 620. Here is exactly how it works in 2026.

The VA Does Not Set a Minimum Credit Score

This surprises a lot of people. The Department of Veterans Affairs guarantees a portion of your mortgage, but it does not require a specific FICO score. VA Circular 26-09-05 explicitly states that credit scores alone cannot be used to deny a VA loan.

In practice, that means the VA looks at your full credit history rather than a single number. Payment patterns, length of credit, derogatory events, and compensating factors all matter.

The problem is that lenders still need to manage their own risk. That is where overlays come in.

What Are Lender Overlays?

An overlay is an additional requirement that a lender adds on top of what the VA requires. Every lender sets their own overlays, and credit score minimums are the most common one.

Some large banks require a 640 or even 660 minimum FICO for VA loans. Other lenders will go down to 580 or lower. The difference has nothing to do with the VA program itself. It is entirely about how much risk the lender is willing to take.

This is why where you apply matters as much as what your score is.

Typical VA Loan Credit Score Minimums by Lender Type

Lender Type Typical Minimum FICO Notes
Big banks (Chase, Wells Fargo) 640 to 660 Strictest overlays, fewest exceptions
Credit unions 620 to 640 Varies by institution
Mid-size mortgage companies 580 to 620 More flexibility, case-by-case review
Mortgage brokers (like Home 1st Lending) As low as 500 to 580 Access to multiple wholesale lenders with different overlays

A mortgage broker can shop your file across multiple lenders at once. If one lender says no at 580, another might say yes with compensating factors. You do not get that option when you apply directly to a single bank.

Which Credit Score Do Lenders Use?

Mortgage lenders pull all three bureau scores (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) and use the middle score. If you are applying with another person, the lender uses the lower of the two middle scores.

Important: the scores used for mortgage lending are FICO models 2, 4, and 5. These are different from what you see on Credit Karma, your bank app, or most free score tools. Those typically show VantageScore 3.0, which can differ from your mortgage FICO by 20 to 80 points in either direction.

Do not assume your Credit Karma score is your mortgage score. It is not.

What If Your Score Is Below 620?

A score below 620 does not automatically disqualify you. It does narrow your lender options and may require compensating factors such as:

  • Low debt-to-income ratio. If your DTI is under 41%, lenders have more room to approve you even with a lower score.
  • Residual income above VA minimums. The VA requires a certain amount of money left over each month after all obligations. Exceeding that threshold is a strong compensating factor.
  • Stable employment history. Two or more years with the same employer or in the same field helps.
  • Cash reserves. Having several months of mortgage payments in savings after closing shows the lender you can absorb a financial hit.
  • Clean recent payment history. If the low score is from old collections or medical debt but your recent 12 months are clean, that matters.

What If Your Score Is Below 580?

This is where it gets harder but not impossible. Very few lenders will approve a VA loan below 580. The ones that do will require strong compensating factors across the board.

If your score is in the 500 to 579 range, here is what I typically recommend:

  • Pull your full credit reports. Look for errors, duplicate collections, or accounts that should have fallen off. Disputing legitimate errors can move your score quickly.
  • Pay down credit card balances. Utilization (the percentage of your available credit you are using) is the fastest lever you can pull. Getting below 30% helps. Getting below 10% helps more.
  • Do not open new accounts. Every new inquiry and new account lowers your average age of credit and can drop your score short-term.
  • Ask about rapid rescore. If you pay down a balance or get a deletion, your lender can request an expedited score update from the bureaus. This can reflect changes in days rather than weeks.

In some cases, 60 to 90 days of targeted credit work can move a score from the low 500s into the upper 500s or low 600s. I have seen it happen dozens of times.

Credit Score vs. Interest Rate

Your credit score also affects your interest rate, even if you qualify for the loan. VA loans do not have risk-based pricing adjustments the way conventional loans do (called LLPAs), but the wholesale lenders pricing VA loans still factor in your credit tier.

Generally:

  • 680 and above: You will see the best available VA rates.
  • 640 to 679: Rates may be slightly higher, typically 0.125% to 0.25% above the best tier.
  • 620 to 639: Rates may be 0.25% to 0.50% higher. Some lenders will require a higher funding fee or additional documentation.
  • Below 620: Limited lender options and rates will reflect the additional risk. Still often better than FHA or conventional at the same score.

Even at a lower credit tier, VA loan rates are typically more competitive than FHA or conventional rates at the same score. The VA guaranty gives lenders confidence that translates into better pricing for you.

The Funding Fee and Credit Score

The VA funding fee is a one-time charge that funds the VA loan program. It ranges from 1.25% to 3.3% of the loan amount depending on your down payment, whether it is your first VA loan, and your service category.

Your credit score does not change the funding fee amount. A veteran with a 580 score pays the same funding fee as a veteran with a 780 score, all other factors being equal. Veterans with service-connected disabilities are exempt from the funding fee entirely.

How to Check Where You Stand Before Applying

Before you apply for a VA loan in Florida, take these steps:

  1. Get your free credit reports at AnnualCreditReport.com. Review all three bureaus for errors or surprises.
  2. Check your FICO scores. If your bank or credit card offers a free FICO score, check it. Know that your mortgage FICO may differ.
  3. Get your Certificate of Eligibility (COE). You can request this through VA.gov or your lender can pull it. This confirms your VA loan entitlement.
  4. Talk to a mortgage broker. A broker can pull your mortgage-specific scores, review your full credit profile, and tell you exactly where you stand across multiple lenders. No guessing.

Common Myths About VA Loan Credit Requirements

"You need a 620 to get a VA loan." Not true. 620 is a common lender overlay, not a VA requirement. Some lenders go lower.

"If one lender rejects you, no one will approve you." Also not true. Different lenders have different overlays. A rejection from a big bank means nothing about your chances with a broker who uses wholesale lenders.

"Collections and charge-offs automatically disqualify you." Not necessarily. The VA allows lenders to approve borrowers with collections if there are compensating factors. Medical collections are treated more leniently than other types.

"You need to wait years after a credit event." Waiting periods after bankruptcy or foreclosure exist, but they are shorter for VA loans than for conventional or FHA loans. Chapter 7 bankruptcy has a two-year waiting period. Foreclosure has a two-year waiting period. Chapter 13 bankruptcy can be approved after 12 months of on-time plan payments with court approval.

Bottom Line

The VA loan program is designed to help veterans buy homes, not to create barriers. There is no VA minimum credit score. Lender overlays are real, but they vary widely, and a mortgage broker can find the right match for your situation.

If your score is below where you want it to be, that does not mean you need to wait years. Targeted credit strategy can move your score meaningfully in 60 to 90 days.
