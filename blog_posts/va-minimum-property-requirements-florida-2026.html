VA Minimum Property Requirements in Florida: 2026 Guide

By Dennis Ross • May 3, 2026

VA loans are not just about the borrower qualifying. The property has to qualify too. That is where VA minimum property requirements, often called MPRs, come in.

For Florida buyers, especially in Orlando and Central Florida, this matters because older roofs, wood rot, pest issues, unpermitted work, missing utilities, and safety problems can delay a closing. A VA appraisal is not a full home inspection, but the appraiser does check whether the home meets VA's basic standard for being safe, sanitary, and structurally sound.

What VA Minimum Property Requirements Actually Mean

VA minimum property requirements are the baseline property standards for a VA-backed loan. The goal is simple: the home should be livable, reasonably safe, and acceptable collateral for the loan.

This does not mean the home has to be perfect. Cosmetic issues usually are not the problem. A worn countertop, dated cabinets, or ugly flooring typically will not kill a VA loan by itself. The bigger concern is whether the property has health, safety, structural, or marketability issues.

Common VA Property Issues in Florida

These are the items I would watch closely on a Florida VA purchase:

  • Roof damage, active leaks, or a roof with obvious remaining-life concerns
  • Wood rot around fascia, soffits, window trim, or exterior doors
  • Peeling paint on homes built before 1978
  • Missing handrails where there is a safety concern
  • Exposed wiring or unsafe electrical conditions
  • Plumbing leaks or missing functional plumbing fixtures
  • No working heat source when required for the property type
  • Broken windows or security issues
  • Standing water, drainage problems, or visible moisture damage
  • Termite or wood-destroying organism concerns
  • Unpermitted additions or work that creates safety or marketability questions

Florida adds its own practical challenges. Heat, humidity, storms, and termites are real factors. A house can look fine in listing photos and still have issues that matter to a VA appraiser, insurance carrier, or underwriter.

VA Appraisal vs Home Inspection

A VA appraisal is not the same as a home inspection. The appraiser is looking at value and whether the property appears to meet VA standards. A home inspector is hired by the buyer to dig deeper into the condition of the home.

Review Purpose What It Does Not Do
VA appraisal Confirms value and basic VA property acceptability Does not replace a detailed inspection
Home inspection Helps the buyer understand condition, repairs, and risk Does not decide loan approval by itself
Insurance review Confirms the property can be insured properly Does not guarantee VA appraisal clearance

Smart buyers do not skip the inspection just because the VA appraisal is coming. The appraisal protects the loan. The inspection protects your decision.

Do VA Loans Require Repairs Before Closing?

Sometimes, yes. If the VA appraiser calls out repairs, those items usually need to be completed and rechecked before closing. That can create timing issues if the seller is slow, the repair requires permits, or the repair affects insurance.

Here is the part buyers need to understand: the VA is not trying to block veterans from buying homes. The program is trying to avoid financing properties with clear safety, health, or structural problems. The issue is not the VA loan. The issue is usually the condition of the property or how the contract was structured.

Who Pays for VA Required Repairs?

That is negotiable unless the contract says otherwise. The seller can pay. The buyer may be able to pay in some situations if allowed by the lender, contract, and transaction structure. Sometimes the agents negotiate a credit, price adjustment, or repair agreement.

Do not assume the seller automatically has to fix everything just because it is a VA loan. Also do not assume the buyer can safely pay for repairs on a home they do not own yet. That needs to be handled carefully before anyone starts spending money.

Properties That Can Be Harder With VA Financing

VA loans can work on many property types, but some files need more attention before you write the offer.

  • Fixer-uppers: Cosmetic updates are one thing. Major safety or structural repairs are another.
  • Condos: The condo project may need to be VA approved. Condo financing also has its own review issues. Read my guide on HOA and condo financing in Florida.
  • Manufactured homes: These require tighter review around eligibility, foundation, age, title, and lender overlays.
  • Homes with additions: Unpermitted rooms, garage conversions, or unclear square footage can create value and safety questions.
  • Rural or well and septic properties: Water, access, septic, and distance requirements may need extra documentation.

How Orlando VA Buyers Can Avoid Delays

The best strategy is to identify property risk before the appraisal is ordered. Once the appraiser calls something out, the file can get boxed into a repair-and-reinspection timeline.

  • Look closely at roof age, visible damage, and insurance concerns before making the offer
  • Ask the listing agent about known repairs, permits, roof work, electrical work, plumbing work, and prior insurance claims
  • Use an inspector who understands Florida property issues
  • Do not ignore small exterior rot or peeling paint on older homes
  • Review condo approval early if the property is a condo
  • Build enough time into the contract for appraisal, repairs, and reinspection if the home has visible issues
  • Work with a loan officer who understands VA loans instead of treating them like a generic conventional file

If you are comparing loan options, read VA loan vs conventional in Orlando. Property condition can be one of the reasons the right loan strategy changes from deal to deal.

What Sellers Should Know About VA Offers

A VA offer is not automatically weaker. That belief costs sellers good buyers. The real question is whether the property is likely to meet VA standards and whether the contract gives everyone enough room to solve issues if they come up.

If the home is well maintained, has working systems, no obvious safety issues, and no major deferred maintenance, a VA appraisal may be straightforward. If the home has visible repair problems, the seller should expect those issues to matter.

Bottom Line

VA minimum property requirements in Florida are not about making the home perfect. They are about making sure the home is safe, sanitary, structurally sound, and acceptable for VA financing.

For Orlando veterans and active duty buyers, the move is simple: get fully preapproved, choose the right property, inspect it carefully, and handle repair risk before it becomes a closing delay.

VA guidelines, lender overlays, insurance requirements, and local property conditions can change. Talk with a licensed mortgage professional before relying on any property strategy for a live purchase contract.
