VA Residual Income Explained for Florida Veterans

By Dennis Ross, NMLS #2018381 | June 17, 2026

VA loans do not approve veterans by debt ratio alone. That is one reason the program is different from a lot of conventional loan conversations. VA also looks at residual income.

Residual income is the money left over each month after the mortgage payment, debts, taxes, and basic obligations are counted. In plain English, VA wants to know if the household has enough room to live after the loan closes.

What VA Residual Income Means

Residual income is not the same thing as cash reserves in the bank. It is a monthly calculation.

A lender starts with qualifying income, then subtracts the proposed housing payment and other counted obligations. The leftover amount is compared against VA residual income tables. Those tables vary by region, household size, and loan amount.

Florida is in the South region for VA residual income purposes. That matters because a veteran buying in Orlando is not compared against the same regional table as a veteran buying in California or New York.

Why VA Cares About Residual Income

Debt-to-income ratio tells part of the story. Residual income tells a different part.

Two buyers can have similar debt ratios and very different real life budgets. A family of five with childcare, auto loans, and insurance costs is not in the same position as a single veteran with the same gross income and fewer obligations.

VA residual income is designed to protect the veteran from getting approved on paper for a payment that does not fit the household.

What Counts Against Residual Income

The details can vary by file and by underwriting findings, but lenders commonly review items like these:

  • The new principal, interest, taxes, insurance, and HOA payment.
  • Monthly debts from the credit report.
  • Child support, alimony, or other required obligations.
  • Federal and state income taxes, when applicable.
  • Maintenance and utility assumptions used in VA underwriting.
  • Childcare costs when they apply to the household.

This is why a pre-approval should not stop at credit score and income. The file has to survive the full VA math.

Household Size Matters

VA residual income uses family size. More people in the household generally means a higher residual income requirement.

That does not mean a larger family cannot qualify. It means the loan officer needs to run the real numbers early. Guessing at the max purchase price without checking household size is how buyers get surprised later.

If dependents, childcare, or support obligations are part of the picture, bring that information up before you start writing offers.

Residual Income and Debt-to-Income Ratio Work Together

VA guidelines allow some flexibility, but flexibility is not a blank check. A higher debt ratio can require stronger compensating factors. Residual income is one of the big items underwriters care about.

If the residual income is strong, it can help support the file. If it is thin, the buyer may need to adjust the plan.

  • Lower the target payment.
  • Pay down or pay off a monthly debt if guidelines allow it.
  • Use seller credits strategically, within VA rules.
  • Compare different insurance quotes before locking in the payment estimate.
  • Review whether the purchase price, taxes, or HOA dues are pushing the file too hard.

Florida Costs Can Change the Calculation

Florida buyers need to pay close attention to insurance, taxes, and HOA dues. Those costs are not side notes. They feed directly into the mortgage payment and can change the residual income picture.

In Orlando and across Central Florida, the same purchase price can produce different payments depending on property taxes, homeowners insurance, flood zone status, condo dues, or HOA fees.

If you are comparing homes, do not compare only the list price. Compare the full payment. A cheaper house with expensive insurance or HOA dues may not be cheaper after underwriting does the math.

How Veterans Can Prepare

Before shopping, get the documents and details that let the lender calculate the file correctly.

  • Recent pay stubs, W-2s, LES, retirement income, or disability income documentation.
  • Full details on monthly debts and any debts being paid off before closing.
  • Estimated childcare costs if they apply.
  • Household size and dependents.
  • Property tax, insurance, HOA, and flood information for any home you are seriously considering.

For more VA planning, read the Florida VA loan guide, the VA funding fee guide, and the VA occupancy requirements guide.

The Bottom Line

VA residual income is one of the most important parts of a Florida VA loan approval. It checks whether the payment still leaves enough monthly breathing room for the household.

The smart move is to calculate residual income before the offer, not after the contract is signed. Guidelines can change, and every file is different. Talk with a loan officer who knows VA loans and can run the full approval picture before you chase a house that only works on a rough estimate.
